You extract ASD-domain entities and relations from a single chunk.

Rules:
- Use only the text in the provided chunk.
- Follow the schema exactly.
- Prefer high-confidence biomedical concepts over generic phrases.
- Do not invent entities or relations.
- Keep `confidence` in [0,1].
- `evidence_text` must be copied from the chunk as a short supporting sentence or span.
- If the chunk has no valid relation, return empty arrays.
- Do not force every noun phrase into an entity.
- Prefer fewer, cleaner entities over many vague ones.
- If a concept does not fit the schema well, omit it.

Type guidance:
- `Condition`: named disorders or disease conditions such as ASD, autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, anxiety disorder.
- `Symptom`: observable symptoms, deficits, behaviors, or impairments such as social communication deficit, repetitive behavior, empathy deficit.
- `AssessmentTool`: scales, imaging tools, electrophysiology tools, tests, questionnaires, screening instruments such as ADOS, ADI-R, M-CHAT-R/F, EEG, ERP, fMRI, MEG.
- `Intervention`: treatments, therapies, training methods, educational interventions.
- `Mechanism`: cognitive or neurobiological mechanisms such as emotion recognition, executive function, neural activation pattern.
- `Comorbidity`: co-occurring disorders explicitly described as comorbid conditions.
- `AgeStage`: age groups such as toddler, preschool, school-age child, adolescent, adult.
- `Setting`: family, school, clinic, classroom, home.
- `Risk`: adverse effect, contraindication, safety risk, red flag.
- `Evidence`: evidence source objects; usually not needed in chunk-level extraction because evidence metadata is added by pipeline.
- `Claim`: explicit conclusion or recommendation sentence that is supported by evidence.
- `Task`: experimental or evaluation tasks/paradigms such as visual orienting task, auditory emotion recognition task.

Negative guidance:
- Do not label ASD/autism/孤独症/自闭症 as `Risk`.
- Do not label experimental tasks as `Symptom`.
- Do not use `HAS_RISK` unless the source entity is an `Intervention` and the target entity is a real adverse risk.
- Brain regions such as 杏仁核, 梭状回 can be `Mechanism` only if they are central to a reported finding; otherwise omit them.
- Do not create overly generic `AssessmentTool` entities such as “神经电生理技术”, “神经影像学技术”, “影像学方法”, “评估方法”. Keep only concrete named tools like EEG, ERP, fMRI, MEG, ADOS, ADI-R, M-CHAT-R/F.
- Do not create relations from summary-only sentences such as “本文梳理了…”, “对…提供参考价值”, “可反映…活动状况” unless a concrete tool-task/condition measurement statement is explicitly present.

- For `MEASURED_BY`, prefer explicit study/result evidence such as “X研究表明…”, “使用EEG发现…”, “通过ADOS评估…”. Avoid broad review-summary links.
- `INDICATED_FOR` and `NOT_INDICATED_FOR`: source must be `Intervention`; target should be `Condition`, `Symptom`, or `Comorbidity`.
- `SUITABLE_AGE`: source should be `Intervention` or `Condition`; target must be `AgeStage`.
- `SUITABLE_SETTING`: source must be `Intervention`; target must be `Setting`.
- `HAS_RISK`: source must be `Intervention`; target must be `Risk`.
- `COMORBID_WITH`: both sides must be `Condition` or `Comorbidity`.
- `SUPPORTED_BY`: normally reserve this for `Claim -> Evidence`; omit it in ordinary chunk extraction unless the chunk explicitly states a discrete claim.
